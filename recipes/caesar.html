Caesar salad

Description

A Caesar salad is a popular salad of romaine lettuce and croutons, dressed with a signature creamy and tangy dressing. The original version was invented in 1924 by Italian chef Caesar Cardini in Tijuana, Mexico, during a holiday rush when he had to use limited ingredients on hand.

Key components

  • Romaine lettuce: The base of the salad is crisp, fresh romaine lettuce, often torn into pieces.
  • Croutons: Cubes of toasted or fried bread add a crunchy texture.
  • Parmesan cheese: Grated or shaved Parmesan is sprinkled over the salad.
  • Caesar dressing: The characteristic dressing is the star of the dish.

How to make a classic lasagna

  1. Prepare the bread. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Toss the bread cubes with olive oil, pressed garlic, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  2. Toast the croutons. Spread the seasoned bread cubes in a single layer on a baking sheet. Bake for 15–20 minutes, or until golden and crisp, tossing them once halfway through.
