Echpochmak

Description

Echpochmak (or öçpoçmaq) is a traditional Tatar and Bashkir triangular pastry with a delicious and juicy filling. A distinctive feature of this dish is that the raw filling is placed inside the dough before baking. During the process, a small hole is left in the center to pour in broth, which keeps the filling moist and tender.

Key components

  • 600 g all-purpose flour
  • 300–400 ml milk
  • 2 eggs
  • 125 g butter, melted
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 11 g dry yeast

How to make a classic lasagna

  1. Make the dough: Combine warm milk, yeast, sugar, salt, melted butter, and eggs. Mix in flour and knead until smooth. Let the dough rise for 1 to 1.5 hours.
  2. Prepare the filling: Finely dice beef (or lamb), potatoes, and onions. Add softened butter, salt, and pepper, and mix well.
  3. Assemble: Roll the dough into small circles. Place a spoonful of the raw filling in the center of each circle.
  4. Form the pastries: Pinch the dough from three sides to create a triangle, leaving a small opening at the top.
  5. First bake: Brush the pastries with egg wash and bake at 190°C (375°F) for 20 minutes.
  6. Add broth and finish: Take the pastries out, pour 1–2 tablespoons of hot broth into each hole, and return to the oven for another 20–30 minutes until golden brown.
  7. Serve: Brush the finished echpochmaks with melted butter before serving warm.
